About the Project

This project is, first and foremost, an act of love for Black Philadelphia and of resistance to the erasure of Black communities in Philadelphia, as in many parts of the world. First conceptualized in 2014, Diggin’ Philly: The Black Philadelphia Project utilizes digital media and technology to document and celebrate the rich experiences, institutions, spaces, and cultures of Black people in the city.

“Black Philly” is a racial-spatial-imaginative construction that captures the lived experiences of Black Philadelphians as well as the increasingly endangered spaces of Black cultural practice in the city. In the spirit of W.E.B. Du Bois’ The Philadelphia Negro, which sought to document the “real condition” of the city’s Black population at the turn of the 20th century, Diggin’ Philly investigates the precarious contemporary conditions of Black Philly amid changing socioeconomic demographics and the systemic displacement of longtime Black residents.

This project operationalizes “diggin/g” as a methodological and analytical praxis. The verb “dig” signifies:

(1) a process of excavation;

(2) the pursuit of a curiosity,

(3) and, in the Black vernacular tradition, a deep appreciation or love for something.

To this end, the various components of Diggin’ Philly utilize geospatial mapping, filmmaking, journalism, archive making, and ethnographic and oral history fieldwork.

Diggin’ Philly: The Black Philadelphia Project serves as one of a growing number of models of community-centered, technologically-mediated, cultural preservation. The project also demonstrates how the tools of the digital and spatial humanities, qualitative research, and media studies can help us more fully understand and teach the lived realities of marginalized populations and spaces.

We dig Philly, and hope you will too.
